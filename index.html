Research

My dissertation, Deflating The Computational Mind, examines the foundations of computational theories of mind, questioning dominant assumptions about how physical systems implement computation. I draw on philosophy of science, metaphysics, and philosophy of biology to develop a more deflationary account of mental computation.

Alongside this project, I work on explainability in AI systems, the role of idealization in computational modeling, and the intersection of philosophy of science with questions about cognition and neuroscience.
